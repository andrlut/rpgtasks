## 0. Cross-store decisions (read first)

### Category recommendation — RECOMMEND: **Lifestyle** (primary)

| Store | Primary | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| **App Store** | **Lifestyle** | **Productivity** |
| **Google Play** | **Lifestyle** | (Play allows one category; use tag set below) |

**Why Lifestyle over Health & Fitness — and why this de-risks review:**

- Apple applies its heaviest scrutiny to **Health & Fitness** and **Medical** apps: Guideline 1.4.1 / 5.1.1 push apps that "provide health-related... services" toward accuracy disclaimers, and apps that could be read as offering psychological assessment risk a request for medical disclaimers or even rejection under the "medical results/diagnosis" clauses. Filing under **Health & Fitness** actively invites the reviewer to evaluate the Big Five / ECR-R / Schwartz / wellbeing inventories as *health instruments*. That is the exact framing we want to avoid.
- **Lifestyle** is the natural home for habit-building, self-improvement, journaling, and personal-growth apps that are *not* medical. It signals "personal development," matching how Perceva actually works (habits + coins + self-reflection quizzes), and keeps the psychometrics read as introspection, not assessment-of-a-condition.
- **Productivity** (secondary, App Store) captures the tasks/quests/streak engine and pulls productivity-intent search traffic without inviting the health-disclaimer path.
- If ASO data later shows Health & Fitness would convert better, it can be switched post-launch — but launch under Lifestyle to minimize first-review friction. The one place *not* to file is Medical.

### Content rating — expected result: **4+ (Apple) / Livre–Everyone (Google IARC)**

The app has no violence, no sexual/suggestive content, no profanity, no gambling, no drugs/alcohol references, no UGC shared between users, no unrestricted web access, no location. The psychometric questions are neutral introspective statements. Item-by-item answers are in section 5.

Aligns with the confirmed collection profile. Full form is a separate deliverable, but the answers both stores need:

| Data type | Collected? | Linked to user? | Used for tracking? | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| App activity (tasks, rewards, skills, quests, XP, streak, logs) | Yes | Yes | No | App functionality |
| Self-reflection responses & scores (Big Five, ECR-R, Schwartz, wellbeing) | Yes | Yes | No | App functionality (personalization/insights) |
| Notification preferences | Yes | Yes | No | App functionality |
| Location, contacts, photos, camera, biometrics, financial info, health/fitness data | **No** | — | — | — |
| Third-party analytics / ads / tracking identifiers | **No** | — | — | — |

- **Apple "Tracking":** No. No ATT prompt needed (no cross-app/third-party tracking).
- **Data deletion:** Yes — declare the in-app account-deletion path (required by Apple 5.1.1(v) and Google's deletion policy; the Edge Function approach the parallel chat is building satisfies both).
- **Encryption in transit:** Yes (Supabase HTTPS). **Data can be deleted:** Yes.
- Do **not** classify any field under Apple's "Health & Fitness" or "Sensitive Info" data categories — the introspection responses go under "Other Data" / "User Content," reinforcing the non-medical framing.
