# A Psicologia do Dinheiro — comportamento > planilha

Ângulo: onde a tese do Housel tem evidência real (experiências de vida moldam
apetite a risco — Malmendier & Nagel; o "gap comportamental" custa ~1,1 p.p./ano
— Morningstar) e onde é aforismo elegante ("enough", riqueza invisível).
Contraponto honesto: a meta-análise de Kaiser (76 RCTs) mostra que educação
financeira também funciona — a leitura justa é "os dois importam". E adaptar ao
contexto BR: os conselhos assumem mercado acionário americano.

## Prompt do Audio Overview (deep dive)

> Focus on money as behavior, not spreadsheets: luck and risk (Groner vs.
> Fuscone), getting rich vs. staying rich, "enough", and the behavior gap
> (investors earn ~1.1pp/year less than their own funds — Morningstar). Then
> push back honestly: the Kaiser meta-analysis of 76 RCTs shows financial
> education does change behavior, so "behavior beats knowledge" is a
> half-truth — both matter. Note the US-market bias for non-US listeners.

## Prompt do infográfico

> Visual guide to The Psychology of Money: the secretary vs. the Merrill Lynch
> executive, luck & risk as twin forces, the behavior gap (fund return vs.
> investor return), getting rich vs. staying rich as different skills, and
> "wealth is what you don't see". Professional style with money-graph motifs.

## Prompt do report

> Executive briefing: the core thesis and its origin essay; the strongest
> evidence-backed claims (experience shapes risk appetite — Malmendier & Nagel;
> the behavior gap — Morningstar); the weakest aphorism-only claims; the Kaiser
> counterpoint on financial education; and what translates (or not) outside the
> US market context.
