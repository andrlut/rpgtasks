# A Vida Boa — o que 85 anos de estudo dizem sobre relações

Ângulo: fitness social como prática. Os números fortes (relações aos 50 prevendo
saúde aos 80 melhor que colesterol; +50% de sobrevivência na meta-análise de 308
mil pessoas) sem comprar o hype — coorte observacional de homens brancos não é
receita universal, e "social fitness" é frame de autor, não achado experimental.

## Fontes pra adicionar no notebook

- https://the-good-life-book.com/ — página oficial do livro (social fitness, modelo W.I.S.E.R.)
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8KkKuTCFvzI — TED do Waldinger (2015, top-10 da história; o notebook extrai a transcrição)
- https://www.adultdevelopmentstudy.org/grantandglueckstudy — site oficial do estudo (268 Harvard + 456 Boston, desde 1938)

- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2896234/ — Waldinger & Schulz 2010 (casais octogenários, efeito-buffer da satisfação conjugal sobre dor)
- https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2017/04/over-nearly-80-years-harvard-study-has-been-showing-how-to-live-a-healthy-and-happy-life/ — Harvard Gazette (melhores números concretos + quote "Loneliness kills")
- https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/the-couch/201706/study-reveals-key-happiness-leaves-question-unanswered — crítica: amostra masculina/branca, "boa relação" sem definição única
- https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2023/11/16/happiness-study-harvard/ — origens problemáticas do estudo e limites reconhecidos pelo próprio Waldinger

## Prompt do Audio Overview (deep dive)

> Focus on what 85+ years of the Harvard Study of Adult Development actually
> shows: relationship satisfaction at age 50 predicting health at 80 better than
> cholesterol; the Holt-Lunstad meta-analysis (50% higher survival odds,
> n=308,849); loneliness as a health risk comparable to smoking; social fitness
> as an active practice, not a given. Stay skeptical: observational cohort,
> originally all-male and white — say "influence", never "proven cause".

## Prompt do infográfico

> Visual guide to The Good Life and the Harvard Study: the 85-year timeline
> (1938 → today, 724 men → 1,300 descendants), the signature finding
> (relationships at 50 beat cholesterol as a predictor), the +50% survival
> meta-analysis, quality over quantity, and social fitness as a workout you
> maintain. Professional style, warm tone.

## Prompt do report

> Executive briefing structured around: the study design and its two cohorts;
> the signature findings; the independent evidence (Holt-Lunstad meta-analysis);
> social fitness and the W.I.S.E.R. model as practice; and an honest limits
> section (observational data, sample composition, survivorship).
